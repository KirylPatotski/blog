I - Sacrifice

In December 2020 there was still a curfew in Germany, that's why I had plenty of time. After a single talk with my parents, they advised me to take action, because I told them, that I want to become an entrepreneur. It was that single event that transformed my life forever. Before this, I wanted to become a nuclear scientist or maybe a physics professor, but on that day, I woke up. My mother found a tutor from Belarus (I speak English, German, Russian, and Chinese) and she helped me to find perspective. She taught me how to get started with programming. My determination was gigantic. At the end of 2020 I was 13 years old and I started to learn how to code. Since learning real programming languages requires an IQ of 200, which I didn't have, I started by completing code tasks for 12 years olds, with JavaScript. code.org I did that for up to 14 hours a day, even though one might say that code tasks for 12-year-olds are below my level, I swallowed my ego and still did it, as it was the only option. After around 3 months I completed every task on that website and on many others and my mind was ready to learn a real programming language, not JavaScript, which is used on this website. But something else happened. I programmed so hard, that I lost interest in programming.

That was no problem. I emigrated from Belarus to Germany in 2013. After another single talk with my mother, she revealed that she learned to speak Germany from scratch in just 3 months. Having heard that, I was shocked, of course, I knew that, but for some reason, I wasn't aware of it, even though I knew that she spoke 5 languages. I went to the bookshop and wanted to find a book on how to learn Chinese üá®üá≥. I knew that I'm going to regret not learning Chinese while I'm young. In the bookshop, I didn't find anything, and that's why I wanted to use an app, Duolingo was the first result, so I picked it. To be fair, other apps offer only useless languages like Italian. Who needs to learn Italian? So right after I had burnout from programming too much, I started to learn Chinese. I finished half the Duolingo course and since I never stopped, I have an 800-day streak and can say quite a lot.

After this purpose was fulfilled, I started to think more long-term. I understood that I need to create content in order to become relevant. In May 2021 I created my first video: Shortcuts on Windows 10. A couple more followed pretty quickly. Before I started to learn how to code I learned how to cook. In 2020 I made at least 130 pizzas. That's why I also tried to make culinary videos. Then, I decided to make more progress and purchased a real video editing software for 30‚Ç¨ (please don't). I made a 5-minute video talking about bits and data (it was without talking, only text with graphics and music). Then I made a second one about - China. I did that for a school project and also uploaded it to YouTube. It failed, because of copyright (I had no idea about copyright, I was 14). That marked a new beginning. I had 2 options I either give up or try something new. The only way to live is to try out something new. I started to create cinematic videos, after seeing this YouTube video. That became relevant much later. It was also the time when I decided to skip 9th grade, that means, I went form 8th grade to 10th grade.

Only a few weeks later my Android programming course started. It was a Belarusian programming course for professional adults; therefore, it was hard. It took up around 12 hours per week from July 2021 to March 2022. I learned a lot and forgot most of it, but there was 1 thing that I learned: how to search the internet properly, an immensely important skill.

At the beginning of 2022, I got my first GoPro, and after the programming course ended, I started to create a massive amount of cinematic, timelapse, and slow-motion YouTube shorts. I created 375 in just 2 months, got half a million views, and learned even more. After the views started to stagnate, because I ran out of ideas, I quit and started working extremely hard again.

This time, I started to work on my first app, Helimath. Originally, I intended to make just a mental math app but then someone gave me the idea to include games. Because ChatGPT didn't exist and I had very little experience in programming I just worked until I no longer could - in the summer holidays for up to 16 hours a day. That's why the first version of Helimath was released on 28.8.2022. In the following months, I continued to work very intensely on my app, because obviously, it was far from done. I also created 70 YouTube shorts documenting my experience, but they also failed. In February 2023 my app could be considered done, and every additional development is only an improvement.

Do you know what's beautiful? In February 2023 I also started going to the gym. In March I started to create YouTube videos, with similar content to my blogs.

II - Focus

In this story, I never talked about friends, women, hobbies, fun, or parties. That's because they didn't exist. I think it's very hard to understand for outsiders how that happened, but one thing is certain. A life like this is rare or not even replicable. This was just the last two and a half years - imagine what's going to happen once I gain more experience and everything will start to compound.

IV - The compound effect

Every small decision leads to big events and decisions. Every time you refuse to be weak, you have made progress. Every time you made progress, you shaped your future and changed it forever. Based on the previous chapter, doing something for as long as 70 absolutely shatters expectations. If you keep investing and learning for 70 years you have no other possibility than becoming extremely successful. The old man would have a completely different life if he never stopped to do sports. Every decision compounds and that means, as soon as you notice a difference, the compound effect has given you enough momentum and is now accelerating harder than ever before.

V - Regret minimization

Let's say I wouldn't have skipped 9th grade, I wouldn't have started to learn Chinese, I wouldn't have started to code. Where would I be now? How could I be looking in the mirror having missed so many opportunities? Visualize yourself at your funeral. Don't just read this text, but try to implement what it says. Visualize every single decision, every significant and insignificant one. And now try to visualize yourself always being brave, hard-working, competent, secure, and with immense reliability. How would your life turn out?

I can't exist simply in regret because regret is a feeling that can't be removed and will only get worse with time. Please take action TODAY, I or you are going to regret it. This could be the day you take control of your life and change it forever. The sooner you take action, the more impact your decisions have.

VI - Struggle as the way to happiness

In North Korean literature, most plots start with suffering, and suffering gets romanticized until the main characters realize that they are happy suffering and that suffering is the only way to happiness. Some people are only happy when they suffer.

Why that's the case? Well, look at regret minimization, the compound effect, living your final life, and, shooting fast and precise.

Suffering means growth, development, resilience, adaptability, and purpose.

If constant growth is the only way to live in order to avoid regret and you need to live your life like you want to live it for the rest of your life, you will need to suffer. Hard things and desirable things take time and pain. And you need to long term and sacrifice.

VII - The only way to live

Every person that is looked up to or legendary never focused on minor things and distracted themselves. These people focused, were fearless, brutally competent, and lived their life to their fullest extent. In order to be happy, you need to get better every day. If you don't try hard enough, you will feel regret.

Look at Felix Baumgarten for example. He shattered the world record for the highest parachute jump in 2012. He went as high as 39km (= 24 mi). That's the perfect example of living life to its fullest extent, without fear, without hesitation, and while growing.

VIII - Why and for what

At the end of the day, what are you living for? Are you living for cheap pleasure or are you living to try to become the best version of yourself and to leave an impact? You need to decide that.

Suffering and growth give you a purpose to exist. It fills your life with something.

IX - Summary

The last 2.5 of my life were a complete sacrifice for later, I lived beyond my comfort zone, constantly learned and did everything extremely hard without any distractions or fun. Imagine living like that for 70 years, every decision will compound. You need to live your life as if it's in the final form and you need to start doing that today, until it's too late, because otherwise, you are going to regret it. Constant growth is the only way to happiness.
