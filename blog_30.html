29.7.2023

Remove micro moments

For the, at the time not too rare occasion of doubt, both external and internal, as well as the recommendation contained in a book, I started to write down a report of every day of my life ever since; I started on April 2nd, 2023. Every day I would write down every significant task I completed/started/resumed that would make me financially free, …

As recently as March 2023 I was still watching YouTube, a direct distraction. But the daily review of the progress was there to hold myself accountable. Accountability is the character trait you need to take control of your life, therefore writing a diary is taking control of your life. Because I got accountable, I started to pressure myself, and - I got such immense results.

But this wasn't the first time I regularly wrote a diary, as a matter of fact in 2019 I wrote 2 pages per day (yes, I myself don't know how I was able to do it) for around 60 days. I used to write diary before I went to bed, as I have written it down back then. I also knew the benefits of doing so at that point in time, when I was 12. There was an event, which made me quit, a party. (Maybe that's why I don't like parties) Because I was 12 my writings were mildly unsophisticated and my font made it impossible to read what the hell I have written.

This essay is not intended to be about writing diaries, even though the title may suggest. This essay is about out-competing 5 people as an individual or completing a week's work in a day, for the rest of your existence, where writing a diary had a key role in helping with this.

Before you get started, you need to make your life perfect. You need to fix your life until perfection. I wrote yesterday's blog “Stay away from me” in one sitting, from 5:30 - 10:30, 3200 words, because I had the mental capacity, health, and IQ to do so. In fact, my lifestyle changes, such as getting up at 5 or taking cold showers led to an IQ increase of around 30 points in just 1 year. You will experience similar changes.

Actually, it took 39 (=7 + 7 + 8 + 17) minutes to speak every video, for some reason, I needed 2 hours. This is exactly where writing a diary helps because it removes this friction. Instead of you procrastinating in front of a 5-minute task and thinking about it all day long, you simply do it. Look, why is it that some people can do genuinely nothing and others outperform 10 people? Obviously, how much order in your life and how good your health is matter extremely, but not less important are micro-moments that you waste. Imagine how many 5-minute tasks, with high leverage, you could do in a day. Probably 0. But now if you remove any procrastination and wasted micro-moments, you could easily do 10.

Essentially, micro moments are moments of such little value, that your conscious mind doesn't even fathom them. Micro-moments are you looking at your clock, which I find to be morally wrong btw., out of the window, at your email program, or maybe at your desk and the bed you have not made? Maybe you have not advanced and your micro-moments are quite significant, for example looking at your phone, or even using the internet to look up a random thought that got into your mind. Maybe you are saying to yourself: “just this song, then I'll start to do something” or you simply procrastinate and wonder why you genuinely completed nothing that day.

Every eleventh grader needed to write something called “KL” which is an 8 paged paper about anything scientific. While everyone complained about how little time they had (just 6 months) I wrote mine in one sitting in 4 hours about Android development. This was in October 2022, before I started going to the gym or ChatGPT existed or I did anything on a great scale, like getting up at 5. In March 2023 I was able to write a 25 paged paper in 2 sittings (about my entrepreneurial journey, and also for school)

The proper way to focus, which removes these micro-moments, is to refuse to physically look away from the work, every time you look away, you lose 5 minutes. Remove distractions as well, it may help you to use airplane mode. Also, get up at 5 and start with the hardest task, because once you start doing it, you will forget what you were scared of in the first place.

It will take a while for you to perfect your life, for me it took 2 years, and as I tried harder, around 3 months. In May I reached a point, where 70% of days looked like straight out of a motivational book, just what I have described here. It had its negative side effects: As my experience, knowledge, and lifestyle started to get even better, everything collapsed in June, at first emotionally (1-7) and then I got to a point where I simply couldn't perform (19-31). I simply ran out of these “scary”, meaningful tasks because I moved too quickly.

You may be tempted to think, that this existence of constant performance is kind of sad. I do acknowledge, I would have thought the same, if I was less advanced. But this is the only way to live. Let's suppose you get rich and successful and influential. Buying a new house or getting a 3-second 12-year-old's glance at your car will become meaningless after like 5 days. What remains after this point? Trying your absolute best does. Having a perfect life provides joy.
