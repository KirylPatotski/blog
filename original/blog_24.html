Time to delete photos

I don't know how you take photos. I don't know if you use a phone, a camera, or even a film camera, a drone, or how you take your pictures, with a tripod, selfie stick, or maybe even free hand. I don't know whether you reserve taking photos for rare occasions or if you take a photo every five minutes. I also don't know whether you are a professional, an amateur, or simply someone who takes photos. I also don't know what you do with your photos.

In my experience, there is one thing that everyone has in common. You probably take too many photos. Even if you take only 1000 photos per year, over the years it compounds into the 10 000s. But there is a large problem with this trend: if you have more photos, their emotional value decreases. And you view them less or don't even view them at all.

In 2018 I was in Belarus, my home country, apparently for the last time, until this point. I took 3 photos. Imagine how valuable these 3 photos are to me now, imagine all the emotional attachment I have to these photos! They are more valuable than any souvenir. That's all I have, potentially the only the 3 photos for the rest of my life.

This vacation I have been to Spain for 2 weeks, a few days prior I got my first photo camera. I took around 3500 photos, and most of them were redundant, blurred, or part of time-lapses. Sorting them out was easy. But now I am left with 1 060 photos of high quality. How should I decide what photos to delete?

Well, here I am going to show you the way how I will/have dealt with large amounts of photos.

The first sieve, so to speak eliminates all images that are blurry, duplicate, overexposed, underexposed, noisy, unclear… Like this, the number of photos should be reduced by half, or even more, if you take photos with a real camera and don't use your phone.

I would say there are 3 types of photos. The irrelevant once, the annoying once, and the good once. The good photos are self-explanatory. They spike emotions or memories of that specific moment and create a bridge to your memories. If I needed to explain annoying photos, here is where it gets interesting because, after the trash photos, which have been eliminated in the first sieve, these photos take up a majority of your photos. These are photos you took because you thought, you would need them later, for someone else, or because you thought that it has artistic value.

I could give you “the 7 best methods to get rid of useless photos” but the problem with these tips is that they are too general and won't give you any more insights. Here is 1 tip, that is able to make a difference. If you have a bunch of photos, view them regularly and every time you view them, try to get rid of a few photos. But I have to warn you! If you start to view your photos regularly, they will lose their magic, so to speak, and your memories will be replaced with the photo itself. That means the real tip is to create as few photos as possible.

P.S. That's the reason, why I have not uploaded photos of Spain to my website for now!
