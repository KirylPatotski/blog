Presence is the key to happiness

Desperate awakening

Why do you fall in love, argue for nothing, procrastinate, and create problems seemingly out of nowhere? Why isn't the world a plain desert but rather a diverse biotope, or at least, why don't people settle in the abundance of space in an empty desert? Why does it rain after all?

Simple, the rain transforms the void desert into a meaningful space, a forest for example, which is suitable for people to settle in and therefore of meaning. So, people crave meaning as the result of the fight against indifference, and the rain itself creates the value and meaning, not the land itself, to capture the meaning, as the land exists in either case, but the absence of rain prevents any further development of the void desert and makes it boring - and by extension worthless.

So, why do you do you fall in love, argue for nothing, procrastinate, and create problems seemingly out of nowhere?

…

Today I heard something that spiked my emotions in a negative sense. When that happens, and here we may differ, I feel a sensation in my stomach area, it feels just like stress, but bundled up into a single moment. While experiencing it, I knew with certainty I would end up documenting this experience. What I heard therefore doesn't matter and it was perhaps ripped out of context anyway. However, there is one thing I noticed intense stress had in common with procrastination and useless arguments, they give a slap from reality and put you back in the current moment. All of a sudden everything else seems to turn indifferent. It's like waking up from a nightmare and realizing that you are all right. It's the craving for awakening caused by mindlessness that's desperately trying to escape, and because it is desperate, it does it through uncomfortable situations. While you may procrastinate all day, be entirely offline, mindless so to speak, the stress caused by procrastination and stress in general try to wake you, to forget about everything else and simply to put you back into the current moment. If you neglect your loved ones, they will start to argue with you to set things straight as a side result, but mostly to remind you of the present moment.

After and during the rain, or the argument in this case, everything else seems to turn indifferent, and unexpected calmness arrives. Just as the quietness, and lull - even more so after a thunderstorm. Useless negative emotions and their spike, therefore, try to gift your mind moments of presence, that it desperately needs, just as the lush forest needs the rain.

Online and offline

Did you really read this text, letter by letter, syllable, by syllable, sentence by sentence, idea by idea, paragraph by paragraph or did you only notice how unexpected this sentence sounds at this very instant? If yes, then what were you doing 1 hour previously or 1 month ago? Most people, grossly overestimate their presence as they never experienced an elevated state of mind continuously, therefore they don't even know that such things are within reach. Especially considering…

Being constantly offline is similar to being constantly online. In both cases, you are living in your world, while offline you are detached from the real world and simply reside within your thoughts. That's the same thing as being completely online, as you have very few thoughts and live in your reality as well, with the only difference being the morph of your reality with the outside, the objective reality visible to outsiders. This phenomenon is similar to the idea of “ego death”, one where you see your body as just a part of the universe and you start to see yourself from some kind of 3rd person, in parts detached from your body. Your soul is free in that case. However, online means total presence, meaning the worlds of your mind and reality match.

What sucks is to be in the middle, in the phase of mixed presence, online and offline at the same time. That's when cracks start to show in your reality, and as a result severe mental suffering such as stress, procrastination, fear, worries, and other things occur. Ultimately, you need to use these cracks to break free, as it's not quite meaningful to live with a deserted mind, one of zero presence. I find it very ironic, that some people (notably those that never meditated for example) claim that I've completely detached from reality. No, I have just arrived at it, I got enlightened. Related to this idea are bad habits. But they don't awaken you, they numb you, that's the difference, and this difference isn't that huge.

What does being stuck in traffic has anything to do with presence, mindfulness, and enlightenment? Nothing. What are you thinking about in those moments, have you ever been present while doing so? The simple answer is no and it is the correct one. Being half-offline and half-online at the same time hurts. Insecurities, stress, and worries reappear. Worries disappear with total presence and the total indifference of bad habits. The concept of mindless time transcends many concepts unrelated, such as meaningless work. That's the reason I believe work-life balance to be a lie. Free time, or time spent without an occupation, such as driving or idle time is time wasted, not spent in presence, but rather in mindlessness, offline. The transition between totally offline and complete presence is the worst state possible. It's easier to prevent the middle if you have a goal - all other people experience immense mental suffering (and they may not even know it).

Presence is one of the many keys to happiness. It removes any negative aspects of the mind and makes you focus on what's important. As discussed previously, presence isn't a fundamental need of the mind, it is the mind itself that wants to communicate with your soul. Reaching mindfulness starts by breathing correctly. Don't breathe shallowly, breathe like a king! Every time you find yourself taking shallow breaths, you have found yourself offline again and fix it. Presence is a skill too. Next to catching offline moments, you need to meditate as well. How do you think you feel after meditation?

The same as after a conflict, except without the negative side effects, faster and more consistent. After doing this for a long while, your body will start to detach from your soul, but that's temporary, as your soul simply shines through for the first time and somewhen, your body and soul will unite again.

Your soul doesn't know fatigue or instant gratification and neither does it know any other negative limitation of the body and the mind. Your soul just exists for itself and this detachment from your body is a first attempt to make you free, your soul takes control, and your body starts to feel like a tool. It becomes impossible to have bad habits, as your body desires instant gratification and not your soul. Negativity, stress, and anxiety fade as well, as this detachment occurs. Are you still breathing correctly? Likewise, total presence, being online is similar to being numbed by bad habits, meaning being offline entirely.

The only way to live

As I was younger, I got sad when I got asked what the meaning of life is. It's simple. Discover everything your mind and soul are capable of, on the side, reaching enlightenment, competence, and enormous material success as a fine side project. There is no doubt, no matter what philosophy may be your favorite, that this path will end up making you the happiest. You won't regret it.

Difficult text, easi(er) summary

Meaning: Meaning is created by the play of order, the desert, and chaos, the rain. The desert is boring as the chaos misses and the chaos creates life itself because it rains.

Desperate awakening: Your mind doesn't want to stand still and wants to create intense feelings, letting it rain so to speak.

Offline: If you are completely numbed, by bad habits, worries and other negatives of the mind disappear, they are swept under the carpet and so is your soul.

Online: Total presence is the key to happiness, as it removes and resolves any negatives in the mind as it frees the soul. It is the highest form of existence; it is hard to obtain.

The middle, a mix of online and offline: Cracks start to show, and your mind starts to suffer. That's why you hate to be stuck in traffic and that's why you try to escape with bad habits to numb yourself and argue for nothing, because you try to achieve presence, through desperation. Your soul tries to break free through desperation or to hide itself in bad habits again.

Your soul: Presence gives access to the soul. Your soul leads to enlightenment and total happiness.

Enlightenment: Your aim for life is to discover what your mind has to offer and to get all the other material things on the side.

Presence is attractive: The bible says, that consciousness is a need, and that's why Eve gave Adam the fruit and sacrificed immortality to become free, to wake up.
